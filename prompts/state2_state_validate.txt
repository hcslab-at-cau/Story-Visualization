## VALIDATION RULES

### PLACE RULES (highest priority)

**RULE 1 — What changes current_place**
Update current_place ONLY when the text explicitly describes a character physically entering, arriving at, or being situated in a new location.
Accepted signal words/phrases: entered, went into, fell into, found herself in, arrived at, came into, was in, landed in, stepped into, came to rest in, had now reached.

**RULE 2 — What does NOT change current_place**
If a character merely sees, notices, looks toward, or aims at a location without physically entering it, record it as mentioned_place ONLY. Do NOT change current_place.
Signal words: saw, looked at, could see, noticed, spotted, longed to go, tried to enter, peeped into, glimpsed, gazed at, peered through, thought about, wondered about.

**RULE 3 — Whole/part spatial hierarchy**
Being at a sub-location (doorway, entrance, gate, edge) does NOT mean being inside the main location (hall, garden, room).
Example: "she found a little door leading into a garden" → mentioned_place = garden, current_place unchanged.
Only promote a sub-location to current_place if the text clearly states the character crossed into the main space.

**RULE 4 — Narrative implicit movement**
The original text is provided in full. Even without explicit movement verbs, if the narrative context makes clear that a physical relocation has COMPLETED (e.g., a long fall ends with "she came to rest on a pile of sticks"), accept the implied location change.
Distinguish carefully:
- Active mid-movement description (falling, running…) → do NOT change current_place yet
- Clear narrative endpoint after movement → current_place change allowed

**RULE 5 — Carry-forward when uncertain**
If evidence is insufficient or ambiguous, carry forward the previous validated current_place unchanged.

### CAST RULES

**RULE 6 — Active cast criteria**
A character remains in active_cast if they are directly mentioned, act, speak, or are otherwise evidenced as present in this paragraph or within the last 1–2 paragraphs.

**RULE 7 — Weak exit**
If a character has had no evidence of presence for 1–2 consecutive paragraphs AND the narrative focus has clearly shifted away, move them to weak_exit_candidates (but keep in active_cast for now).

**RULE 8 — Hard exit**
Remove a character from active_cast entirely ONLY if:
- The text explicitly states they left, went elsewhere, or are absent, OR
- They are described as being in a clearly different location

### NON-NARRATIVE PARAGRAPHS

**RULE 9 — Non-narrative carry-forward**
If is_narrative is false (chapter heading, section break, etc.), carry forward the previous validated state unchanged. Set actions to [].

Process frames in pid order. Each frame's validated_state must be consistent with the previous frame's validated_state. When in doubt, carry forward.
